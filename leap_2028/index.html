What is LEAP 2028?

Leading Environmental Action Plan (LEAP) is our comprehensive 3-year roadmap to achieve carbon neutrality, build climate resilience, and create a sustainable economic future for our community.

  • Copper Mining

    Chile has some major problems with climate change and pollution. Chile is the world's largest producer of copper and mines it, as well as other metals such as lithium and iodine, along the Andes mountain range. The mines are in a poor condition, and the machinery used contributes hugely to climate change. Therefore, the first year of our three year plan is to clean up copper mining. Copper is essential to our way of life, as we use it in wiring, devices and all sorts of electric cars. Speaking of electric cars, in the first year of the three year plan, we will try to encourage (force) car companies to move away from oil cars and start producing electric and hybrid cars.

    Manufacturing in Chile

    Chile also has some huge problems with manufacturing. It contributes a significant portion of the GDP and exports, and is Chile’s main way of making money. However, the main products they manufacture are processed foods, (which produce a lot of greenhouse gases) chemicals, which often leak and wreak havoc to the local environment, and paper and wood products, which contribute hugely to deforestation. Therefore, in the second year of our plan, we are going to shut down the processed foods factories, which will also improve overall health and make the govornment some money, which we can use to clean up the chemical production. Also, we will prosecute any logging companies who cut down trees over their quota, and we will make it compulsory for all companies to plant at least as many trees as they chop down. Also, for the second part of our electric car plan, we will make it illegal to drive a petrol or diesel car, unless you have special dispensation from the govornment.

  • Agriculture and Fishing

    Chile has rich agricultural and fishing resources that form an important part of its economy. However, when fishing plays a key part in your economy, overfishing tends to happen. To prevent this, we will make sure all fishers are properly registered, and monitor the amount of fish they catch, and prosecute them if they overfish, or go over their allotted quota. We will also strongly discourage trawling along the seabed, as it can be harmful to coral life. As well as this, we will prosecute anyone who says ‘67’, not for any environmental reasons, but simply to prevent any uneducated swine from entering the country. Finally, for the last part of our electric car plan, we will use all of the money we made to put up solar panels and electric car charging points to charge the electric cars.
